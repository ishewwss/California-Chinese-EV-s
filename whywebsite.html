Why Create a Website for This Project?

For this Multi‑Modal Project, I chose to build a website because it allows the information to be presented in a way that feels modern, interactive, and easy to explore. Instead of reading a long essay all at once, a website lets people move through the ideas at their own pace. Each page focuses on a specific topic, which makes the information clearer and more organized.

A website also gives the project a more realistic feel. Electric vehicles, especially Chinese EVs, are part of a fast‑growing tech industry, so presenting the research on a digital platform makes sense. It matches the topic. Websites are how most people learn about new technology today, so using this format helps the project feel more connected to the real world.

Another reason I chose a website is because it allows visuals to play a bigger role. Photos of EVs, charging stations, and modern interiors help the reader understand what makes these cars appealing. Images break up the text and make the information more engaging, especially for people who prefer visual learning.

Finally, creating a website shows digital skills that go beyond writing. It involves design, organization, layout, and creativity. Being able to communicate information online is an important skill today, and building this site helped me practice that. Overall, a website makes the project more interactive, more modern, and more effective at explaining why Chinese EVs matter in California’s future.
